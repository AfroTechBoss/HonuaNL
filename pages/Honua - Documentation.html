Honua design documentation

Honua is the social network for people fixing the planet — familiar feed-and-follow mechanics with a sustainability soul baked into every post, profile, and interaction. This document covers the design system, information architecture, every screen, and the three new features that anchor the redesign.

01

Vision & goals

The previous Honua skewed niche and tool-like. The redesign keeps the values but adopts the conventions people already know from mainstream social apps, so a first-time user is never confused about how to post, follow, or browse. The sustainability layer is what makes it different — never what makes it harder.

Familiar by default

Feed, follow, compose, profile, DMs, notifications — the patterns are conventional so adoption is frictionless.

Impact, made visible

Every post and person carries a measurable, verified impact signal. Good action is legible, not vague.

From scrolling to doing

The feed routes people to real-world action — local projects, challenges, and offsets — not just more content.

Trust through verification

Oracle- and third-party-verified impact and on-chain carbon traceability separate Honua from greenwashing.

Green is the single brand colour, used sparingly for action and identity. Sky signals verification and data. Sun marks streaks and warmth; Clay is reserved for fire/streak iconography and destructive actions. Everything else is a warm near-neutral. A full dark theme is defined on the same tokens.

Iconography

One lucide-style line set, 1.75 stroke (2+ when active). Never decorative — icons label or act.

Voice

  • Specific over vague. “20 panels, −80% footprint” beats “made my home greener”.
  • Numbers in mono. Data reads as fact, not flourish.
  • Action verbs, present tense. “Compost food scraps”, not “Try composting”.

05

New features

Three flagship additions, plus the cross-cutting impact layer that ties the whole product together.

NEW · 01
Personal impact dashboard

Your environmental footprint as a living ledger — finally visible, measurable, and motivating.

  • KPI cards: CO₂ avoided, trees funded, water saved, energy cut — each with month-over-month deltas.
  • 30-day CO₂ ledger chart, stacked by category (transport, energy, food, other).
  • Streaks & levels: daily streak with a 30-day 2× multiplier; XP levels (e.g. “Level 7 · Composter”).
  • Verified actions list and a neighbourhood ranking leaderboard.
NEW · 02
Local action map

Turns intent into attendance. Find a cleanup, garden, repair café, or co-op near you this weekend.

  • Split view: filterable project list beside an interactive map with category pins.
  • Filters by type (cleanup, garden, energy, repair, policy, waste) and search by host or tag.
  • Per-project attendance (“84 going”), host, and schedule; “Start a project” entry point.
  • Area-impact summary aggregating hours, waste removed, and trees planted nearby.
NEW · 03
Carbon credit marketplace

Offset what you can’t avoid — every credit on-chain verified, third-party audited, and traceable to a real project.

  • Live market: spot price, 24h volume, projects listed, your yearly offsets.
  • Credit table with type, region, verifier (Verra, Gold Standard, Puro.Earth…), vintage, price, and availability.
  • “Subscribe & forget” auto-offset that matches your tracked footprint monthly.
  • Optional soulbound impact NFT minting your year on-chain.

The impact layer (cross-cutting)

Three primitives appear everywhere and make Honua feel different from a generic social app:

Sustainability score

A 0–100 signal on every post and person; verified actions raise it. Surfaced as the score pill.

Verified impact

Oracle- and third-party-checked figures wear a sky badge, so claims are trustworthy.

Rewards economy

Green Points (GP) from actions, Impact Tokens (IT) for the carbon market, and Honua governance.

Rewards reference. GP — earned per logged/verified action (e.g. bike commute +24 GP, renewable plan +200 GP); fuels levels & streaks. IT — Impact Tokens spent in the carbon market. HONUA — governance token, earned at higher tiers.
06

Interactivity & flows

The desktop prototype is fully interactive: every primary control resolves to a real navigation, modal, or state change. Three global systems power this — a router with parameters, a modal layer, and a toast layer — backed by persistent state in localStorage (likes, saves, follows, joined communities & challenges, cart, wishlist, drafts, and theme survive reload).

Global systems

SystemBehaviour
NavigationClicking a post, person, project, article, or notification routes to the right screen with params (e.g. post id, profile handle). Back/forward and reload preserve position.
ModalsDetail & action overlays — post compose, product, carbon credit/buy, project, article reader, challenge, discussion, tip, wallet, badge, celebration, edit profile, create challenge/project, new collection, export, delete-account. Esc / backdrop closes.
ToastsTransient confirmations bottom-centre — success (post published, saved, joined) and error (offline → saved to drafts) with optional undo / view-drafts actions.
Optimistic stateLike, save/bookmark, follow, RSVP, join, and cart counts update instantly and persist.

Key flows

Compose & drafts
New-post (sidebar or composer) opens the composer; publishing shows a success toast. Toggling “simulate offline” saves to drafts and raises an error toast with a recovery action.
Post engagement
Cards open the detail screen; like / repost / bookmark / share and threaded replies all work, with author follow and clickable avatars to profiles.
Home tabs
For you / Following / Verified impact filter the feed (Following keys off who you follow; Verified shows oracle-verified posts) with empty states.
Carbon market
Category pills filter the table; rows & Buy open the credit modal with a quantity-priced purchase that ends in a celebration. Wallet, Offset-my-year, and Auto-offset each open dedicated modals.
Action map
List rows and map pins open the project sheet (RSVP, message host, share); “Start a project” opens a creation form.
Marketplace
Products open a detail modal with quantity + add-to-cart; wishlist and cart are live counts; Wishlist/Cart buttons open their contents.
Notifications
Each notification routes to its target — likes/comments → the post, follow → the profile, project → the map, streak → challenges + a celebration, badge/level → profile + a badge modal.
Communities
About and Join/Leave (with undo), “I’m going” RSVP, and discussion threads all resolve; the cover now sits behind the avatar & name.
Challenges
Create challenge, Log today (celebration), and Join/Leave with a per-challenge detail modal.
Profile
Your own profile shows Edit (opens an editor) & Share; other people show Message, Tip, and Follow. Achievements open badge detail.
Settings
All nine sections switch in place. Delete account is a two-step confirm — warning, then type-to-confirm “delete my account”. Appearance toggles a real dark theme.
07

Using the prototypes

Two clickable prototypes merge the screens into navigable apps so you can feel the real flow before build.

Desktop

Full-window app with browser chrome. Click any sidebar item to move between all 14 screens; the URL bar updates and back/forward work. Your last screen is remembered on reload.

Mobile

Phone shell that scales to fit. The bottom tab bar navigates the core screens; the floating compass button opens a “jump to screen” menu covering all 8 screens, including sign-in and onboarding.
